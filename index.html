About me

I am an Electrical Engineer, Computer Scientist, and now a (3rd year PhD student) Computational Linguist and Phonetician. My B.Sc. and M.Eng. training in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with concentration in Artificial Intelligence (AI) have equipped me with the skills to ideate, prototype, implement and deploy to production large, often customized Data Science, Machine Learning (ML) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) pipelines to clients (see employment at Virtualitics, inc). However, I believe the success of Large Language Models (LLMs) at Natural Language Tasks has shifted us away from building specialized neural architectures and training regimes to specific applications of the pretrained representations they are able to learn.

In order to effectively leverage these pretrained embeddings in natural language applications, a comprehensive domain expertise is required. As a Computational Linguist, I am able to ask pointed questions about natural language datasets, identify specific linguistic phenomena of interest to the context or application at hand, and uniquely characterize the data in useful ways, such as with syntactic, sociolinguistic historical linguistic or phonetic context. I am able to develop concrete hypotheses about the data, analyze large bodies of text and conduct experiments to falsify or prove my hypotheses. My linguistic skills additionally allow me to conduct in-depth failure analyses of LLMs in testing and deployment. (see Vázquez Martínez et. al. 2023; Vázquez Martínez, 2021).

Finally, my specialization in Phonetics in conjunction with my Electrical Engineering skills have uniquely enabled me to perform acoustic and digital signal processing analyses on audio corpora, empowering me to work on speech processing and any LLM applications to it such as Text2Speech or Speech2Text.
